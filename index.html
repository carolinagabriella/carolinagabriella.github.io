Reflection on Managing the Learning Environment

This unit has provided me the opportunity to reflect upon my own practices and theories as a preschool teacher. I was able to reflect on the building blocks needed to construct a positive classroom environment and how to piece them together properly in order to achieve it. Working alongside our cohort has also allowed me to benefit from the experiences of other teachers, enriching my knowledge and sparking new ideas for future strategies in the classroom.

One key takeaway that sticks out for me is the importance of being adaptable. Successfully running an inclusive classroom means you may have to unexpectedly modify procedures and activities for one or more students. Keeping this in mind, I have learned the importance of proper planning as way to more efficiently utilize time and keep goals clear. Like we have discussed together, investing time in proper planning will prevent you from wasting time in the classroom.

This brings me to another repeating key point that we talked about throughout this unit, consistency. As a preschool teacher, consistency with expectations, rules and classroom norms helps to establish a child’s social and emotional development. Consistency allows no room for guessing and generates a clear path to expectations, aiding in more predictable behavior from students.

Both proper planning and consistency facilitate routine, creating a sense of stability withing the classroom. From most of our experiences, and especially now as we work virtually, we can all attest that these two key practices are crucial. However, this unit has also served as a reminder to be observant and considerate.

Sometimes it can be easy to trust our first instinct and forget that each child is not only complex but constantly changing, as well. This unit has prompted me to remember that it is not about “having all the answers” but instead about learning where and how to search for them. Our students must be understood in order to be reached. Monitoring the triggers, getting to know their interests and learning how to consistently adapt our classroom in order to help overcome their obstacles together is what it is all about.
